About

The slightly longer version.

Game programming student — targeting C++ roles in engines, gameplay systems, and graphics.

I’m Milo — most of what I want to show employers lives in C++: a custom engine (Helicon), Vulkan rendering, and Unreal gameplay. I still ship Unity / C# when it’s the right tool, but I’m deliberately growing toward professional native code and engine-style problems.

What I’m like to work with

  • C++-minded — I care about ownership, clear boundaries between systems, and not paying for abstractions you don’t need.
  • Comfortable low in the stack — engine loops, graphics APIs, profiling — I’ll read the disassembly if that’s what it takes to understand a hitch.
  • Still collaborative — designers and other programmers have to live with my code; I write and commit like they’re the audience.

What I want next

An internship or junior role where C++ is the day-to-day language — engine, gameplay systems, tools, or rendering. I want to learn from people who’ve shipped big codebases and earn my spot on a team that values solid systems work.
